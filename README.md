## Blog Post 1
Studyspark, your personal study buddy. What started as a prototype in my User Experience Design, Prototyping, and Evaluation class is now on it's way to reality.

Over the last few months my team and I were working on creating the idea and UI design of an app called StudySpark. It all stared after we interviewed some of our fellow peers on their study habits. One thing stood out. Most students know they should do the studying, they want to do it, but when they actually sit down. It just doesn't click. Learning often feels passive, repetitive, and honestly kind of draining. 

So we set out to understand why students struggle with engaging in their learning outside of class. More importantly we wanted to create a tool that would increase engagement in studying and overall retention of information. 

That is how Studyspark started, what happened after conducting our interviews led us to create the foundation of the app. 

First we introduced the idea of bite-sized sessions. Think Duolingo, Mimo, Quizlet, Anki. Everyone we conducted interviews with consistenlty reported that large tasks like studying often feel daunting causing a lake of motivation. Our design breaks work into short and manageble sessions. 

Secondly we created the idea of visbile progress. Students noted they were more motivated when they saw advancement. So we focused on ahving a design with clear progress indicators and milestone feedback. Thinking of this in a user sense: it is great and awesome for users to see their progress. Now on the side of business this is also great and awesome. This allows users to keep coming back to the app because they are "winning" through progress they see visual which keeps them coming back. 

Thirdly, users wanted an enganing experience. Like I aaid earlier studying for our peers often felt passive repetitive, our design needed to focus on making learning interactive, stimulating and rewarding.

Last but not least(Fourth): Users wanted a clear content structure. It is important how material is presented directly due to its affect on peoples focus and retention.

To summarize I am creating an app that allows to learn in bite-sized sessions with visual progress that teaches you in a clear structure that allows you to stay enganged.

Moving forward we will be talking about the integration of these ideas in the app through:
- AI-generated learning roadmaps
- Bite-sized modules and submodules
- AI micro-lectures that rewrite any concept in plain language
- Auto-generated quizzes with instant feedback
- Streaks and points
- A leaderboard
- Saved history across sessions

And much more.
